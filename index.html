Impression and Explanation of Tableau Visualization

The data set I chose was "Top US Baby Names in US, 1910-2012", from the Tableau Public Data Sets. This visualization tool does a fantastic job of making a lot of data understandable. Learning the tool and seeing how to make pleasing visualizations was very fulfilling and rewarding. The tree map I made worked out, since I had a lot of data that needed to be simplified in a digestible manner. By having a large excel data sheet or .csv file (with this data set, for example), it can make your data easily interpreted and pleasing to look at for a viewer. The reason I chose this tree map of the Top Baby Names in the US from 1910-2012, is beacuse out of the different visualization tools (pie chart, histogram, heat map, line chart, etc) they would accomplish the task of showing the number of occurences of each name over the time period, it just did not compare to the simplicity of a tree map, which makes the squares larger or smaller depending on a number of occurences, which makes it very simple to understand at a glance. The user can also hover their mouse over the boxes, and view the exact count, or whatever other measurement they choose to include in the visualization via Tableau. This is why this tool is fantastic for accomplishing this task.
